The Types Of Albums

Of course, albums come in many shapes and forms. There are 3 types of albums that are prevalent in the music industry that I would like to share with you.

Although these are all albums, they're quite different from one another, and I'm not talking only in their genre. They're also very much different in the purpose of their creation.

Compilation Albums
Concept Albums
Experimental Albums

Compilation Albums

Let's start off with compilation albums. As in the name implies, compilation albums are quite literally a compilation of the musician's songs from various recording projects. They mostly don't follow any "grand" themes, with its often only theme and purpose being the artist's "greatest hits". Songs included on a compilation album may be previously released or unreleased tracks, usually from various recording sessions by one or several performers.

Example: Thriller by Michael Jackson

Concept Albums

Concept albums is an interesting one. Its described as an album that holds meaning or larger purpose when the tracks are put together collectively than individually. This is usually achieved through establishing a single narrative or a theme, which can be lyrical, compositional or instrumental. Though the term is more associated with rock music, other genres have managed to pick it up as a result of its popularity and use of expression.

Example: IGOR by Tyler, The Creator

Experimental Albums

Experimental albums, or experimental music, is a label for any music or music genre that pushes pre-defined boundaries or genre definitions. The broad definition of "experimental" is an opposition to, and questioning of institutionalized musical compositional, performing, and aesthetic traditions.

Example: Kid A by Radiohead
